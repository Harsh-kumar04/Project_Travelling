Punducherry

The calm and colourful Union Territory of Puducherry lies on India's southern coast. A former French colony, it is known for age-old monuments, majestic churches, exotic beaches, and tree-lined boulevards.

Attractions:
-Promenade Beach
-Auroville
-Sri Aurobindo Ashram
-French Quarter
-Paradise Beach
-Matrimandir
-Botanical Garden


Tips:
->Visit during October to February
->Respect local customs and traditions
->Wear lightweight clothing and sunscreen
->Use mosquito repellent

How to reach?

Certainly, here's a concise overview of how to reach Puducherry:

You can reach Puducherry by:
Air: ,
The nearest major airport is Chennai International Airport. From there, you can take a taxi or bus to Puducherry.
Train:
Puducherry has its own railway station with connections to Chennai, Bangalore, and nearby cities.
Bus:
State-run and private buses connect Puducherry to Chennai, Bangalore, and other towns.
Road:
Puducherry is accessible by car via well-maintained highways.
Within Puducherry, use local options like auto-rickshaws and taxis for getting around.
